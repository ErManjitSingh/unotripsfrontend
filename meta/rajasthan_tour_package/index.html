About Rajasthan & Ladakh Tour Packages

Rajasthan — India's Land of Kings — is one of North India's most captivating destinations. From the pink-hued palaces of Jaipur to mirror lakes in Udaipur and golden dunes outside Jaisalmer, Rajasthan blends royal heritage, vibrant bazaars, folk music, and desert adventure. UNO Trips designs Rajasthan packages for every traveller — couples on romantic lake-city escapes, families exploring forts safely, and explorers chasing camel safaris and tiger reserves.

Jaipur is the gateway: Amer Fort, Hawa Mahal, City Palace, and colourful Johari Bazaar set the tone for any itinerary. Jantar Mantar and Nahargarh Fort add astronomy and sunset panoramas. Most tours begin here before heading south to lakes or west toward the Thar Desert — the perfect base for a well-paced Rajasthan circuit.

Udaipur — the City of Lakes — pairs marble palaces with boat rides on Lake Pichola and sunset views from Sajjangarh. Jag Mandir and the old city lanes reward slow evenings; nearby Kumbhalgarh and Chittorgarh forts extend the Mewar story for travellers with extra days.

Jodhpur and Jaisalmer complete the desert arc: Mehrangarh Fort rising over the blue city, then the golden fort and dunes where camel camps and folk performances turn nights magical. Pair with Bikaner or a Ranthambore safari for wildlife and fewer crowds on longer routes.

Across the state, painted havelis, stepwells, and desert villages preserve Rajputana craft and cuisine. UNO Trips coordinates heritage hotels, trusted drivers, skip-the-line fort entries, and safari bookings — so whether you are planning a Jaipur honeymoon, a family Rajasthan holiday, or a desert camp adventure, you get transparent pricing and ground support from arrival to departure.

Rajasthan travel FAQs

Quick answers for season, heritage sites, desert safaris, and travel tips—before you book your Rajasthan trip with UNO Trips.

What is the best time to visit Rajasthan?
October to March is ideal for desert safaris and pleasant sightseeing across Jaipur, Jodhpur, and Jaisalmer. July to September brings greenery in Udaipur and Mount Abu. Summer (April–June) is hot — plan fort visits early morning. UNO Trips helps you pick the right season for your route.
Which cities should I include in a Rajasthan trip?
A classic circuit covers Jaipur (Pink City palaces), Udaipur (lakes and romance), Jodhpur (blue city and Mehrangarh Fort), and Jaisalmer (golden fort and Thar Desert). Add Pushkar, Ranthambore, or Bikaner for spirituality, wildlife, and desert charm.
What should I pack for a Rajasthan holiday?
Light cottons for day, a warm layer for desert nights (Nov–Feb), comfortable walking shoes for forts, sunglasses, hat, and sunscreen. Carry modest clothing for temples and palaces. For desert camps, pack a light jacket.
Is Rajasthan safe for families and solo travellers?
Rajasthan is one of India's most visited states with excellent road connectivity and 4G in major cities. Stick to registered guides and licensed safari operators. UNO Trips provides verified drivers, pre-booked stays, and 24/7 on-ground support.
Do I need advance booking for forts and desert safaris?
Popular slots at Amber Fort, Ranthambore tiger safaris, and palace hotels fill fast in peak season (Oct–Mar). Book at least 2–4 weeks ahead. UNO Trips handles safari windows and heritage hotel reservations.
